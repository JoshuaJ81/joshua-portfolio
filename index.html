About Me

And then some.

  • Joshua Lavallais, MBA

    My name is Joshua Lavallais. I am a creative spirit, and have and always will love art. For me, designing and building web pages is “digital art.” To date, I primarily work with HTML, CSS, Flexbox, CSS Grid. I specialize in creating web pages that are mobile responsive, user friendly, all while using the right typography and color schematic. I am currently learning JavaScript and Adobe Illustrator. Like everyone else, I have a story. My web designer journey is as follows below.

  • Sometime ago in the year 2018

    It all started with WordPress

    I launched a blog site called Aquarius Ink, (which I later renamed to Quiet Momentum) using the world-renowned content management system, Word Press. With help and guidance from experts, I learned to build and custom web pages using built-in tools, particularly WP Bakery. I quickly realized that I loved designing and building web pages, such that I could do so for hours. Case in point, I would sit for hours on the weekends, building my website, while often ignoring the hunger pangs in my tummy, as I was too engaged to step away to eat. However, I never thought I could learn how to code. But then, that all changed.

  • Summer of 2020

    From Quiet Momentum to building my own custom web pages

    As I continued creating content for Quiet Momentum, a friend introduced me to a YouTube course which covered the basics of web design...it was exciting! I quickly realized I loved designing and building web pages “using code.” Upon finishing the course, I felt the need to learn more, but from whom or where? Subsequently, I took my time researching different bootcamps and programs. I wanted to do what was best for me. And then...

  • January 2022

    Enter CodeCademy

    Per the advice of a seasoned web designer, I enrolled in classes provided by Codecademy. Since then, I have learned many skills. Also, I have learned from outside sources such as W3 Schools, MDN, and Stack Overflow. Learning to code is a lifelong journey. Methods and frameworks will change; thus, it is important to stay abreast of all that is current. Some people may view any continuous learning cycle as a “tedious endeavor,” but in life, each of us must strive to learn and commit to the role of “lifelong student” in the game of life. Learning and acquiring knowledge is eternal. My goal, overall, is to learn and become better at my craft, while delivering a product that is completely satisfactory to both the client and me.
